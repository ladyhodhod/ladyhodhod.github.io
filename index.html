    <h2>About Me</h2>

    <p>I am an Associate Teaching Professor and Associate Area Head of the Information Systems program at <strong>Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar (CMU-Q)</strong>. My research focuses on <strong>Natural Language Processing (NLP), Generative AI, and Arabic Dialect Processing</strong>, with a broader interest in building equitable AI systems for morphologically complex and low-resource languages.</p>

    <p>I received my PhD in Computer Science (Computational Linguistics) from <strong>Université Paris-Sud 11</strong> in France. Over the past decade, I have led and collaborated on large-scale projects in <strong>Arabic NLP</strong>, including the <em>Qatar Linguistic Map</em>, the <em>MADAR project</em>, and the first <em>Automated Arabic Essay Scoring systems</em>.</p>

    <p>I am also passionate about <strong>teaching and mentorship</strong>. At CMU-Q, I design and teach courses spanning application development, data science, and NLP, while supervising undergraduate and graduate theses.</p>

  </section>

  <section>

    <h2>Selected Projects</h2>

    <ul>

      <li><strong>Dimension-Specific Automated Scoring of Arabic Essays (2023–2026)</strong> – Developing the first automated multi-dimensional essay scoring system for Arabic writing proficiency.</li>

      <li><strong>TartanBotQ (2024–2025)</strong> – Designing a university advising chatbot for CMU-Q students using conversational AI.</li>

      <li><strong>Qatar Linguistic Map (2020–2024)</strong> – Large-scale initiative documenting and analyzing Qatari dialect variation.</li>

      <li><strong>MADAR Project (2017–2020)</strong> – Created one of the largest multi-dialectal corpora and lexicons for Arabic dialect processing.</li>

    </ul>
