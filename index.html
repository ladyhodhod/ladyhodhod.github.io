About Me

I am an Associate Teaching Professor and Associate Area Head of the Information Systems program at Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar (CMU-Q). My research focuses on Natural Language Processing (NLP), Generative AI, and Arabic Dialect Processing, with a broader interest in building equitable AI systems for morphologically complex and low-resource languages.

I received my PhD in Computer Science (Computational Linguistics) from Universit√© Paris-Sud 11 in France. Over the past decade, I have led and collaborated on large-scale projects in Arabic NLP, including the Qatar Linguistic Map, the MADAR project, and the first Automated Arabic Essay Scoring systems.

I am also passionate about teaching and mentorship. At CMU-Q, I design and teach courses spanning application development, data science, and NLP, while supervising undergraduate and graduate theses.
